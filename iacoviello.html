01 — Problem Statement

Apa yang Ingin Dipecahkan?

Paper ini mengajukan dua pertanyaan fundamental tentang pasar perumahan AS:

Q1 Nature of Shocks

Apa saja shock yang menggerakkan pasar perumahan? Apakah housing market hanya pasif merefleksikan aktivitas makro, atau justru menjadi driving force siklus bisnis?

Q2 Spillover Size

Seberapa besar spillover dari housing market ke ekonomi yang lebih luas? Khususnya, bagaimana fluktuasi housing wealth mempengaruhi konsumsi non-housing?

Mengapa penting? Pengalaman boom-bust perumahan AS awal 2000-an menunjukkan bahwa housing bukan sekadar aset pasif. Model yang hanya melihat satu sektor gagal menangkap interaksi harga rumah → borrowing capacity → konsumsi. Iacoviello & Neri membangun DSGE dua-sektor dengan heterogeneous agents untuk menangkap mekanisme ini.
02 — Model Features

Anatomi Model DSGE

Model ini merupakan perluasan dari New Keynesian DSGE standar (Smets-Wouters type) dengan empat elemen kunci yang ditambahkan:

i Multi-Sector Structure

Dua sektor produksi: nonhousing (konsumsi + business investment menggunakan capital & labor) dan housing (new homes menggunakan capital, labor, land, & intermediate input). Ini memunculkan endogenous relative price dynamics.

ii Nominal Rigidities

Calvo-style sticky prices di sektor konsumsi + sticky wages di kedua sektor (dengan partial indexation). Harga housing bersifat fleksibel — karena housing mahal per unit dan dinegosiasikan individual.

iii Household Heterogeneity & Collateral Constraints

Dua tipe household: patient (lenders, \(\beta = 0.9925\)) dan impatient (borrowers, \(\beta' = 0.97\)). Impatient households menghadapi borrowing constraint yang terikat pada nilai rumah. Inilah mekanisme utama spillover.

iv Rich Shock Structure

9 shock eksogen: 3 technology (nonhousing, housing, investment-specific), 3 preference (intertemporal, housing demand, labor supply), dan 3 monetary/nominal (i.i.d. monetary, inflation objective, cost-push).

03 — Deep Dive: Heterogenitas Agen

Bagaimana Agent Heterogeneity Dimodelkan?

Ini adalah fitur inti yang paling relevan untuk tesis Anda. Iacoviello & Neri membangun heterogenitas melalui perbedaan discount factor yang menghasilkan pembagian endogen antara lenders dan borrowers.

Patient Households (Lenders)

Discount factor \(\beta = 0.9925\) (lebih sabar). Mereka mengakumulasi capital, housing, dan land; meminjamkan dana ke impatient households dan firms.

Keputusan: \(c_t, h_t, k_{c,t}, k_{h,t}, k_{b,t}, l_t, n_{c,t}, n_{h,t}, z_{c,t}, z_{h,t}, b_t\)

Income share (\(\alpha\)): ~79% of labor income (estimated)

Impatient Households (Borrowers)

Discount factor \(\beta' = 0.97\) (lebih tidak sabar). Tidak memiliki capital atau land. Hanya mengakumulasi housing dan meminjam maksimum terhadap nilai rumahnya.

Keputusan: \(c'_t, h'_t, n'_{c,t}, n'_{h,t}, b'_t\)

Income share: ~21% of labor income → 17% of consumption

Mekanisme Kunci: Collateral Constraint

BORROWING CONSTRAINT
$$b'_t \leq m \, E_t \left[ \frac{q_{t+1} \, h'_t \, \pi_{t+1}}{R_t} \right]$$
Hutang maksimum impatient household = LTV ratio × expected present value rumah. Karena \(\beta' < \beta\), constraint ini selalu binding di sekitar steady state.
Mengapa \(\beta' < \beta\) menjamin binding constraint? Dengan impatience yang cukup besar, impatient agents akan terus mendekumulasi kekayaan hingga lower bound — yaitu batas yang ditentukan collateral constraint. Untuk "small shocks", mereka selalu berada tepat di batas ini. Model dilinearisasi di sekitar steady state dengan constraint yang binding.

Bagaimana Heterogenitas Menghasilkan Spillover?

Mekanisme transmisi bekerja melalui rantai berikut:

Shock Positif (misal: housing demand ↑)
\(j_t\) naik → housing lebih diinginkan
Harga Rumah Naik: \(q_t \uparrow\)
Housing price merespons sebagai forward-looking variable
Collateral Value Naik
\(b'_t \leq m \cdot E_t[q_{t+1} h'_t \pi_{t+1}/R_t]\) → borrowing capacity ↑
Borrowers Meningkatkan Konsumsi
\(c'_t \uparrow\) karena MPC borrowers tinggi (discount factor rendah)
Aggregate Consumption \(C_t \uparrow\)
Meski lender \(c_t\) turun sedikit, efek net positif karena MPC borrowers > MPC lenders
Kunci kuantitatif: Tanpa collateral effect (\(\alpha = 1\)), housing demand shock menurunkan konsumsi (crowding out). Dengan collateral (\(\alpha = 0.79\)), respons konsumsi positif. Elastisitas konsumsi terhadap housing wealth naik 2.5 pp (dari 0.108 ke 0.133) berkat mekanisme ini.

Labor Market Heterogeneity

Kedua tipe household menyuplai labor ke kedua sektor. Dalam production function, jam kerja keduanya masuk secara Cobb-Douglas:

COMPOSITE LABOR
$$\text{Labor input} = (n_{i,t})^\alpha \, (n'_{i,t})^{1-\alpha}, \quad i \in \{c, h\}$$
Parameter \(\alpha\) mengontrol labor income share sekaligus economic size masing-masing grup. Ini memberikan complementarity antar skill.

Preferensi: Perbedaan Antar Agen

Utility function keduanya memiliki struktur sama, tapi parameter bisa berbeda:

Patient

Habits: \(\varepsilon = 0.32\) (rendah)
Disutility labor: \(\eta = 0.52\), \(\xi = 0.66\)

Impatient

Habits: \(\varepsilon' = 0.58\) (tinggi — kompensasi ketiadaan capital smoothing)
Disutility labor: \(\eta' = 0.51\), \(\xi' = 0.97\)

05 — Key Equations

Daftar Persamaan Utama

A. Utility Functions

PATIENT HOUSEHOLD UTILITY
$$E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty} (\beta G_C)^t \, z_t \left[ \Gamma_c \ln(c_t - \varepsilon c_{t-1}) + j_t \ln h_t - \frac{\tau_t}{1+\eta} \frac{\left(n_{c,t}^{1+\xi} + n_{h,t}^{1+\xi}\right)^{\frac{1+\eta}{1+\xi}}}{1} \right]$$
IMPATIENT HOUSEHOLD UTILITY
$$E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty} (\beta' G_C)^t \, z_t \left[ \Gamma'_c \ln(c'_t - \varepsilon' c'_{t-1}) + j_t \ln h'_t - \frac{\tau_t}{1+\eta'} \frac{\left({n'}_{c,t}^{1+\xi'} + {n'}_{h,t}^{1+\xi'}\right)^{\frac{1+\eta'}{1+\xi'}}}{1} \right]$$
Log-log preferences reconcile trending relative prices with stable expenditure shares. \(\xi, \xi' > 0\) implies imperfect labor mobility across sectors (Horvath 2000).

B. Budget Constraints

PATIENT BUDGET CONSTRAINT (B1)
$$c_t + \frac{k_{c,t}}{A_{k,t}} + k_{h,t} + k_{b,t} + q_t h_t + p_{l,t} l_t - b_t = \frac{w_{c,t} n_{c,t}}{X_{wc,t}} + \frac{w_{h,t} n_{h,t}}{X_{wh,t}} + \left(R_{c,t} z_{c,t} + \frac{1-\delta_{kc}}{A_{k,t}}\right) k_{c,t-1}$$ $$+ (R_{h,t} z_{h,t} + 1 - \delta_{kh}) k_{h,t-1} + p_{b,t} k_{b,t} - \frac{R_{t-1} b_{t-1}}{\pi_t} + (p_{l,t} + R_{l,t}) l_{t-1} + q_t(1-\delta_h) h_{t-1} + Div_t - \phi_t - \frac{a(z_{c,t})}{A_{k,t}} k_{c,t-1} - a(z_{h,t}) k_{h,t-1}$$
IMPATIENT BUDGET CONSTRAINT (B12)
$$c'_t + q_t h'_t = \frac{w'_{c,t} n'_{c,t}}{X_{wc',t}} + \frac{w'_{h,t} n'_{h,t}}{X_{wh',t}} + b'_t - \frac{R_{t-1} b'_{t-1}}{\pi_t} + q_t(1-\delta_h) h'_{t-1} + Div'_t$$
Impatient households memiliki budget constraint yang jauh lebih sederhana — tanpa capital accumulation dan land.
COLLATERAL CONSTRAINT (B13)
$$b'_t = m \, E_t \left[\frac{q_{t+1} \, h'_t \, \pi_{t+1}}{R_t}\right]$$
Holds with equality karena \(\beta' < \beta\). Parameter \(m = 0.85\) mengukur likuiditas housing wealth.

C. Key First-Order Conditions

  1. Housing Demand — Patient (B2)
    $$u_{c,t} \, q_t = u_{h,t} + \beta G_C \, E_t\left[u_{c,t+1} \, q_{t+1}(1-\delta_h)\right]$$
  2. Euler Equation — Patient (B3)
    $$u_{c,t} = \beta G_C \, E_t\left[\frac{u_{c,t+1} \, R_t}{\pi_{t+1}}\right]$$
  3. Housing Demand — Impatient (B14)
    $$u_{c',t} \, q_t = u_{h',t} + \beta' G_C \, E_t\left[u_{c',t+1} \, q_{t+1}(1-\delta_h)\right] + \lambda_t \frac{m \, q_{t+1} \pi_{t+1}}{R_t}$$
    \(\lambda_t > 0\) adalah Lagrange multiplier dari borrowing constraint — the "price of tightness"
  4. Euler Equation — Impatient (B15)
    $$u_{c',t} = \beta' G_C \, E_t\left[\frac{u_{c',t+1} \, R_t}{\pi_{t+1}}\right] + \lambda_t$$
    \(\lambda_t\) muncul sebagai "wedge" — impatient agents tidak bisa smooth consumption karena constraint binding
  5. Capital in Nonhousing (B4)
    $$u_{c,t}\left(\frac{1}{A_{k,t}} + \frac{\partial \phi_{c,t}}{\partial k_{c,t}}\right) = \beta G_C \, E_t\left[u_{c,t+1}\left(R_{c,t+1} z_{c,t+1} - a(z_{c,t+1}) + \frac{1-\delta_{kc}}{A_{k,t+1}} - \frac{\partial \phi_{c,t+1}}{\partial k_{c,t}}\right)\right]$$
  6. Land Pricing (B11)
    $$u_{c,t} \, p_{l,t} = \beta G_C \, E_t\left[u_{c,t+1}(p_{l,t+1} + R_{l,t+1})\right]$$

D. Production Technologies

NONHOUSING SECTOR (Eq. 6)
$$Y_t = A_{c,t} \left(n_{c,t}^\alpha \, {n'}_{c,t}^{1-\alpha}\right)^{1-\mu_c} \left(z_{c,t} \, k_{c,t-1}\right)^{\mu_c}$$
HOUSING SECTOR (Eq. 7)
$$IH_t = A_{h,t} \left(n_{h,t}^\alpha \, {n'}_{h,t}^{1-\alpha}\right)^{1-\mu_h-\mu_b-\mu_l} (z_{h,t} \, k_{h,t-1})^{\mu_h} \, k_{b,t}^{\mu_b} \, l_{t-1}^{\mu_l}$$
Land (\(l\)) sebagai fixed factor → membatasi housing supply → house prices bisa naik secara sekuler.

E. Phillips Curves

PRICE PHILLIPS CURVE (Eq. 8 / B28)
$$\ln\pi_t - \iota_\pi \ln\pi_{t-1} = \beta G_C \left(E_t \ln\pi_{t+1} - \iota_\pi \ln\pi_t\right) - \varepsilon_\pi \ln\left(\frac{X_t}{X}\right) + u_{p,t}$$
\(\varepsilon_\pi = \frac{(1-\theta_\pi)(1-\beta G_C \theta_\pi)}{\theta_\pi}\). Estimated: \(\theta_\pi = 0.83\) (reoptimize every ~6 quarters), \(\iota_\pi = 0.69\).
WAGE PHILLIPS CURVES (B29–B32)
$$\ln\omega_{i,t} - \iota_{wi} \ln\pi_{t-1} = \beta_{type} G_C \left(E_t \ln\omega_{i,t+1} - \iota_{wi} \ln\pi_t\right) - \varepsilon_{wi} \ln\left(\frac{X_{wi,t}}{X_{wi}}\right)$$
4 wage equations: for each sector \(i \in \{c, h\}\) × each household type. Wage stickiness in housing (\(\theta_{wh} = 0.91\)) lebih tinggi dari consumption (\(\theta_{wc} = 0.79\)). Sticky wages krusial — tanpa ini, residential investment tidak sensitif terhadap monetary shocks.

F. Taylor Rule & Shock Processes

TAYLOR RULE (Eq. 9 / B33)
$$R_t = R_{t-1}^{r_R} \, \pi_t^{(1-r_R)r_\pi} \left(\frac{GDP_t}{G_C \cdot GDP_{t-1}}\right)^{(1-r_R)r_Y} \bar{r}^{\,1-r_R} \frac{u_{R,t}}{s_t}$$
\(r_R = 0.59\) (interest rate smoothing), \(r_\pi = 1.44\) (inflation response), \(r_Y = 0.52\) (output growth response). \(s_t\) captures persistent shifts in inflation target.
TECHNOLOGY PROCESSES (Eq. 12–15, balanced growth)
$$G_C = 1 + \gamma_{AC} + \frac{\mu_c}{1-\mu_c}\gamma_{AK}$$ $$G_q = 1 + (1-\mu_h-\mu_b)\gamma_{AC} + \frac{\mu_c(1-\mu_h-\mu_b)}{1-\mu_c}\gamma_{AK} - (1-\mu_h-\mu_l-\mu_b)\gamma_{AH}$$
Real house prices naik jika \(\gamma_{AH} < \gamma_{AC}\) — yaitu jika teknologi housing tertinggal. Estimated: \(\gamma_{AC} = 0.32\)%, \(\gamma_{AH} = 0.08\)% per quarter → menjelaskan kenaikan sekuler harga rumah riil.

G. Market Clearing

GOODS MARKET (B34)
$$C_t + \frac{IK_{c,t}}{A_{k,t}} + IK_{h,t} + k_{b,t} = Y_t - \phi_t$$
HOUSING MARKET (B35)
$$h_t + h'_t - (1-\delta_h)(h_{t-1} + h'_{t-1}) = IH_t$$
LOAN & LAND MARKETS (B36)
$$b_t + b'_t = 0, \qquad l_t = 1$$

Total system: 36 equations in 36 variables: \(c, h, k_c, k_h, k_b, n_c, n_h, b, l, z_c, z_h, c', h', n'_c, n'_h, b', IH, Y, q, R, \pi, \lambda, X, w_c, w_h, w'_c, w'_h, X_{wc}, X_{wh}, X_{wc'}, X_{wh'}, R_c, R_h, R_l, p_b, p_l\).

06 — Metode Estimasi

Bayesian Estimation

Data

10 observables, 1965:Q1–2006:Q4:
Real consumption, residential inv., business inv., real house prices, nominal interest rate, inflation, hours (×2 sectors), wage inflation (×2 sectors).

Method

Linearize around balanced growth path → state-space model → likelihood via Kalman filter → combine with priors → Metropolis-Hastings algorithm (500,000 draws, ~25% acceptance rate).

Alur Estimasi

1. Data Transformation
Keep trends, remove levels. Demean inflation, interest rate, hours, wage inflation. Allow measurement error for housing sector hours & wages.
2. Calibrate Subset
Fix \(\beta, \beta', j, \mu_c, \mu_h, \mu_l, \mu_b, \delta_h, \delta_{kc}, \delta_{kh}, X, X_w, m, \rho_s\) to match steady-state ratios
3. Set Priors
Beta for bounded [0,1] params, Gamma for positive params, Normal for unbounded, Inv-Gamma for shock SDs
4. Metropolis-Hastings
Random walk MH with covariance = Hessian at posterior mode. 500K draws. Convergence checked by splitting sample in half.
5. Analysis
Impulse responses, variance decomposition (HP-filtered), historical decomposition, subsample estimation (pre/post 1980s)

08 — Mapping ke Tesis

Relevansi untuk Tesis Anda

Tesis: Regional Economic Linkages, Recovery Dynamics, and the Macroeconomic Effects of Rare Natural Disasters: A DSGE Analysis with an Application to the Indonesian Context

Analogi Struktural

Iacoviello & Neri (2010)

  • 2 agent types: patient vs impatient
  • Heterogeneity via \(\beta \neq \beta'\)
  • Collateral constraint ties housing → borrowing
  • 2 sectors: housing vs nonhousing
  • Spillover: housing wealth → consumption
  • Housing preference shock as demand driver
  • Land as fixed factor limiting supply
  • Subsample: financial liberalization (changing \(m\))

Tesis Anda (Potensi Adaptasi)

  • 2 region types: disaster-affected vs non-affected
  • Heterogeneity via productivity/capital destruction
  • Constraint: damaged infrastructure → reduced production capacity
  • 2+ sectors: tradable vs nontradable (atau multi-region)
  • Spillover: regional disaster → national consumption/output
  • Disaster shock as rare, large negative shock
  • Regional fixed factors (geography, infrastructure)
  • Policy counterfactuals: subsidy vs transfer (changing policy params)

Pelajaran Kunci dari Model Ini

1 Discount Factor Heterogeneity → Natural Borrower/Lender Split

Anda bisa mengadaptasi ini: disaster-affected region bisa memiliki discount factor efektif yang lebih rendah (karena urgent reconstruction needs), membuat mereka net borrowers dari region lain. Ini secara natural menghasilkan interregional capital flows.

2 Binding Constraint = Amplification Mechanism

Collateral constraint yang always-binding adalah kunci amplifikasi. Untuk bencana: constraint bisa berupa reconstruction capacity (limited labor/materials) atau fiscal constraint (limited government budget). Saat bencana merusak collateral (infrastructure), borrowing turun → output turun lebih jauh.

3 Sectoral Heterogeneity + Fixed Factor = Endogenous Relative Prices

Land dalam model ini bisa dianalogikan dengan regional infrastructure atau geographic endowment. Fixed factor menghasilkan endogenous scarcity → price dynamics yang realistis. Setelah bencana, scarcity construction materials/labor → harga rekonstruksi naik.

4 Shock Persistence Matters Enormously

Housing pref. shock dengan \(\rho_j = 0.96\) menghasilkan efek sangat persisten pada harga. Untuk bencana: capital destruction bersifat one-time tapi recovery bisa sangat lambat tergantung reconstruction speed — ini menjawab RQ4 Anda tentang speed of recovery.

5 Bayesian Estimation Framework

Metropolis-Hastings dengan 500K draws, clear prior specification, dan convergence diagnostics. Framework ini bisa diadopsi langsung, meski untuk rare disaster Anda mungkin perlu calibration-heavy approach karena disaster events jarang muncul di data.

Adaptasi Heterogenitas Agen untuk Multi-Region

Ide inti: Ganti patient/impatient households dengan disaster-affected/non-affected regions. Masing-masing region memiliki production function sendiri, tapi terhubung via trade (intermediate goods) dan financial markets (interregional lending). Disaster shock menghancurkan capital di satu region → relative price changes → spillover ke region lain via trade linkages dan financial constraint tightening.
POSSIBLE ADAPTATION: REGIONAL PRODUCTION
$$Y_{i,t} = A_{i,t} \, K_{i,t-1}^{\mu_i} \, N_{i,t}^{1-\mu_i}, \quad i \in \{\text{affected}, \text{non-affected}\}$$
Disaster shock: \(K_{\text{aff},t} = (1 - \Delta_t) K_{\text{aff},t-1}\) di mana \(\Delta_t\) adalah fraction of capital destroyed.
POSSIBLE ADAPTATION: INTERREGIONAL TRADE LINKAGE
$$C_{i,t} = \left[\omega_i^{1/\sigma} (C_{i,t}^{local})^{(\sigma-1)/\sigma} + (1-\omega_i)^{1/\sigma} (C_{i,t}^{import})^{(\sigma-1)/\sigma}\right]^{\sigma/(\sigma-1)}$$
CES aggregation of local dan imported goods → trade elasticity \(\sigma\) controls spillover magnitude.
